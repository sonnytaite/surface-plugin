# Selection rubric — what is worth sharing

> version: 0.1 (yours to edit — bump the version when you re-weight)
>
> /share scores digest candidates against this file. When what you *pick* keeps
> diverging from what this rubric *ranks*, that divergence is the signal to edit this
> file — your picks are the real rubric; this is its current written approximation.

## Dimensions (score 1–5 each)

| Dimension | 5 looks like | 1 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **Foundational** | would change how a teammate works or thinks | trivia; nothing follows from it |
| **Named** | a coined, reusable concept ("the X pattern") | an unnamed observation |
| **Connected** | high in-degree; other pages lean on it | an orphan page |
| **Evidenced** | grounded in real work, results, or sources | assertion only |
| **Transferable** | a peer could apply it without my context | only makes sense in my head |

Centrality is computed, not guessed (in-degree over the live link graph).

## Hard gates (kill/hold — they do not just dock points)

- **Stage gate.** Every candidate carries its stage (*thought piece / vision / options
  explored / prototype / in production*). Fails ONLY if the piece would need to dress
  the idea up a rung to be worth telling. Stage-as-it-is always passes.
- **Sensitivity tier.**
  - `share-now` — fine for anyone, including outside the team.
  - `team` — for the team; the normal tier for internal foundational work.
  - `hold` — not for sharing yet: live negotiations, personal data, security detail,
    anything your `domain-rules.md` restricts. A held idea can still be listed for
    YOU in a private held-note; it never enters a shareable digest.
  - When in doubt between `team` and `hold`, ask the owner — do not silently hold
    foundational work, and do not silently share edge cases.

## Tie-breakers

Prefer: the idea that unblocks the most other ideas; the one a teammate asked about
this month; the one with a working artefact behind it (a pack beats a brief).
