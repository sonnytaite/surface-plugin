# Domain rules — boundaries the verifier cannot infer

> Yours to fill in. These are the org-specific, regulatory, or contractual boundaries
> that a fresh reader (or a fresh AI) would not know. The fidelity-verifier treats each
> rule here as a BLOCK-level check on every outward draft.
>
> Write each rule as a checkable statement. Good rules are specific enough that a
> verifier can point at a sentence and say "this violates rule 3".

Examples of the *kind* of rule that belongs here (replace with your own):

- Never imply our system spans organisational boundary X — our scope is Y only.
- Product Z is approved for use cases A and B only; never describe wider use.
- A human reviews and accepts every AI output in workflow W — never describe W as
  autonomous.
- Do not name vendors we are in live negotiation with.
- Do not include numbers from internal financial or workforce planning documents.
